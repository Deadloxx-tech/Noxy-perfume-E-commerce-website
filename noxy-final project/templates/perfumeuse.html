How to Use and Maintain Different Types of Perfumes

Floral Perfumes

Floral perfumes are light and refreshing, ideal for daytime wear. They evoke the scent of fresh flowers and are perfect for spring or summer occasions.

  • Apply to pulse points like wrists and neck
  • Store in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight
  • Use sparingly to avoid overpowering the scent

Woody Perfumes

Woody perfumes offer earthy, masculine notes and are great for evening or formal events. They provide a long-lasting, sophisticated aroma.

  • Spray on clothing or hair for extended wear
  • Keep in a dry environment to preserve the base notes
  • Layer with complementary scents for depth

Citrus Perfumes

Citrus perfumes are vibrant and energizing, with fresh, zesty notes. They're excellent for casual wear and can uplift your mood instantly.

  • Apply in the morning for a refreshing start
  • Avoid heat exposure to prevent scent evaporation
  • Use as a body mist for light, all-day fragrance

Oriental Perfumes

Oriental perfumes are rich and exotic, featuring warm spices and resins. They're ideal for special occasions and create a luxurious, lingering presence.

  • Dab on skin for intimate, close-range scent
  • Store upright to maintain the oil concentration
  • Pair with warm clothing for enhanced projection

Fresh/Aromatic Perfumes

Fresh aromatic perfumes blend herbal and green notes for a clean, natural feel. They're versatile and suitable for everyday use in any season.

  • Spray liberally for a burst of freshness
  • Refrigerate during hot weather to extend shelf life
  • Experiment with different application areas for variety
